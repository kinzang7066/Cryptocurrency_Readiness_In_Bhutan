## 📌 About the Project
A survey-based data analysis project examining whether Bhutanese adults are ready to adopt cryptocurrency. The project explores digital payment behavior, crypto awareness, and readiness levels across different groups in Bhutan — and maps findings to Bhutan's Gross National Happiness (GNH) values.

## 🔑 Key Findings

- Bhutan is **80% ready** — average readiness score of **12.75 out of 16**
-  **96%** of respondents have heard of cryptocurrency but understanding remains basic
-  **Poor internet** is the biggest barrier to crypto adoption
-  **Age** is a stronger predictor of readiness than location
- Semi-Urban respondents aged **35-54** scored the highest at **15 out of 16**
-  Urban residents aged **55+** scored the lowest at **7.8 out of 16**

##  Data Limitations

- Most respondents are young (18-24) and from Urban areas — rural voices are underrepresented
- Sample size of 113 is relatively small for a country-wide conclusion
- Responses are self-reported and may be influenced by misunderstanding or social bias
- Findings are a starting point — not a final conclusion

##  Readiness Score Explained

The Readiness Score (out of 16) is calculated by combining 4 survey questions:

| Question | Measures | Max Score |
| Q13 Digital Familiarity | Education | 4 |
| Q15 Crypto Understanding | Knowledge | 4 |
| Q18 Feel Ready | Confidence | 5 |
| Q19 Would Adopt Crypto | Willingness | 3 |
| **Total** | **Overall Readiness** | **16** |

##  Business Recommendations

1. **Run crypto awareness campaigns** targeting groups with low knowledge but high smartphone access
2. **Simplify user experience** — design crypto tools that minimize fear of mistakes
3. **Build public trust** — clear communication about security and consumer protection
4. **Start with small businesses** — encourage shops to accept crypto to normalize its use
5. **Improve internet infrastructure** — especially in rural areas
