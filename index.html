💡 Recommendations

5 Solutions to Improve
Crypto Readiness in Bhutan

Based on 113 survey responses, these five targeted solutions address the real barriers identified in the data — from education gaps to scam vulnerability.

01
🎓

Close the Education Gap

Run targeted crypto literacy programs for 35-54 and 55+ age groups. Young people need digital platforms. Older generations need community workshops and face to face education in local languages.

02
🌐

Fix Internet Infrastructure First

Poor internet is barrier number one. The government must expand reliable and affordable internet to rural and semi-urban areas before pushing crypto adoption. Connectivity is a prerequisite — not a luxury.

03
🛡️

Protect Vulnerable Groups from Scams

The 35-44 group has the highest trust and lowest security concern — the perfect target for scammers. Launch dedicated scam awareness campaigns with simple clear messaging about red flags and how to stay safe.

04
🏛️

Government Must Lead on Governance

People are already asking for government support. Create clear cryptocurrency regulations, establish consumer protection laws, and build a transparent framework. Strong government leadership is the single most powerful accelerator of adoption in Bhutan.

05
🤝

Start With Communities — Let Adoption Grow Naturally

The majority said Maybe — not No. These undecided people are waiting to see crypto work in real life. Encourage local shops and small businesses to accept crypto as payment. When people see their favorite restaurant using crypto safely — it becomes real, relatable and trustworthy. Change starts in communities, not government offices.

Research Results

Key Findings

Four critical insights from 113 survey responses

01
🏆

Bhutan is 80% Ready

Average readiness score of 12.75 out of 16 — Bhutan shows strong foundational readiness for cryptocurrency adoption aligned with GNH values.

02
👂

High Awareness, Low Understanding

96% have heard of cryptocurrency but most only have basic understanding — a critical knowledge gap exists between awareness and true readiness.

03
🌐

Internet is Barrier #1

Poor internet connection is the single biggest barrier to adoption — especially critical for rural communities who remain underserved.

04
📅

Age Predicts Readiness

Age is a stronger predictor of readiness than location — younger groups show consistently higher scores regardless of where they live.
